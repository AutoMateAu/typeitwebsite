What Is an AI Receptionist for Real Estate?

An AI receptionist is a voice-based system powered by artificial intelligence that answers your agency's phone line, understands what the caller needs, and takes action — whether that's capturing a seller lead, booking a property inspection, answering a rental enquiry, or routing an urgent call to the right agent.

This isn't the "press 1 for sales, press 2 for property management" phone tree that callers hang up on. Modern AI receptionists use natural language processing to have real conversations. A caller can say "I'm looking to sell my three-bedroom house in Paddington and want to know what it might be worth" and the AI captures their details, asks the right qualifying questions, and sends the lead to the appropriate agent — instantly.

Here's what a well-configured AI receptionist handles for a real estate agency:

  • Answering every inbound call 24/7, including after hours, weekends, and during Saturday open homes
  • Capturing and qualifying seller leads — property address, timeline, motivation, and contact details
  • Handling buyer enquiries — property availability, inspection times, price guides, and booking viewings
  • Managing rental and property management calls — maintenance requests, lease enquiries, arrears follow-ups, and tenant communications
  • Routing calls intelligently — sending seller leads to the listing agent, maintenance issues to the property manager, and urgent matters to the principal
  • Booking property inspections and appraisals directly into your calendar or CRM
  • Answering common questions — agency hours, agent availability, current listings, recent sales, suburb information
  • Sending SMS follow-ups with property details, inspection confirmations, or agent contact information
  • Providing call summaries and transcripts so no lead detail is ever lost

What it doesn't replace is the relationship-building, negotiation, and deal-making that makes a great agent. It handles the front door so you can focus on closing.

How AI Receptionists Work for Sales and Property Management

What makes real estate different from other industries is that your agency likely runs two distinct businesses under one roof: sales and property management. A good AI receptionist handles both.

For the sales team

  • Seller lead capture: When a potential vendor calls asking for an appraisal or wanting to know what their property is worth, the AI captures their details — property address, bedrooms, bathrooms, timeline, contact information — and immediately alerts the relevant agent
  • Buyer enquiries: Callers asking about specific listings get property details, price guides, and inspection times. The AI can book them into upcoming open homes or private inspections
  • Appraisal bookings: Sellers ready to meet with an agent get booked directly into the listing agent's calendar
  • After-hours lead capture: The calls that come in at 7pm when someone's browsing realestate.com.au on the couch — these are high-intent leads that most agencies send to voicemail

For property management

  • Maintenance requests: Tenants call to report a leaking tap or broken air conditioner. The AI captures the details, categorises the urgency, and logs it in your system — or escalates genuine emergencies (burst pipe, no hot water, security issue) to a human immediately
  • Rental enquiries: Prospective tenants calling about available properties get answers about rent, inspection times, application processes, and pet policies
  • Landlord communications: Owners calling about their investment property get professional handling and messages routed to their property manager
  • Arrears follow-up: AI can make outbound calls to tenants with overdue rent, providing a consistent and professional touchpoint without your team spending hours on the phone

How to Choose an AI Receptionist for Your Real Estate Agency

Integration with Australian real estate CRM and property management software

This is the non-negotiable. Your AI receptionist needs to work with the software your agency already runs:

  • Rex Software — one of Australia's leading real estate CRMs, used by 2,500+ agencies for sales, rentals, and marketing. Your AI needs to push leads, log calls, and sync with Rex's contact database.
  • PropertyMe — used by over 6,000 agencies and one in two property managers across Australia and New Zealand. Critical for maintenance request logging, tenant communications, and trust accounting workflows. PropertyMe recently acquired Phoenix CRM to offer a combined sales and PM platform.
  • MRI Property Tree — a leading cloud-based property management platform with strong trust accounting and automation features, developed by Rockend (now part of MRI Software).
  • VaultRE — comprehensive CRM with strong customisation, scalability, and mobile capabilities.
  • Reapit — established platform covering both sales and property management, with 20,000+ users across Australia and New Zealand.
  • Console Cloud — property management focused, widely used across Australian agencies.

If the AI can't push a seller lead into Rex or log a maintenance request in PropertyMe, it's just a fancy answering machine. The whole point is eliminating manual data entry so your team acts on leads immediately.

Understanding real estate-specific call types

Real estate calls are uniquely varied. Your AI needs to distinguish between — and correctly handle — very different caller intents:

  • A vendor wanting an appraisal (high-value lead — capture everything and alert an agent immediately)
  • A buyer asking about a specific listing (provide information, book an inspection)
  • A tenant reporting a maintenance issue (log it, categorise urgency, escalate emergencies)
  • A landlord asking about their rental income (route to property manager)
  • A solicitor calling about a settlement (route to the relevant agent or admin)
  • A cold caller trying to sell you something (handle politely without wasting agent time)

A generic AI receptionist will treat all of these the same. A real estate-configured one knows the difference and acts accordingly.

Australian voice, local knowledge, and property terminology

Real estate is intensely local. If your agency covers the Northern Beaches and a caller asks about Dee Why, your AI should sound like it belongs there — not in a call centre overseas. Natural Australian voices and the ability to handle local suburb names, property terminology (strata, body corporate, settlement, exchanged, under offer, EOI, expressions of interest), and market-specific language is essential for building trust with callers.

Multi-agent and multi-department routing

Most agencies have multiple agents, each with their own listings, landlord portfolios, and areas of responsibility. Your AI receptionist needs to route calls intelligently — sending a buyer enquiry about a specific listing to the listing agent, a property management call to the right PM, and a general enquiry to whoever is available. Smart routing turns your AI from a message-taker into a true front desk. See how Entry routes calls to the right person.

Common Concerns from Principals and Agents

"Real estate is a relationship business. You can't automate that."

You're right — and nobody's suggesting you automate the relationship. What you automate is the first 60 seconds: answering the phone, capturing the caller's needs, and getting the right information to the right agent. The relationship starts when you call that qualified lead back within five minutes with their full details already in your CRM. That's a better first impression than voicemail.

"My agents prefer to answer their own phones."

Great agents should be spending their time listing, selling, and negotiating — not playing phone tag. The AI handles calls when agents can't pick up (inspections, auctions, meetings, driving) and routes qualified leads with full context. Your agents still run the relationships; they just never miss the opening.

"What about sensitive negotiations or vendor-confidential information?"

A well-configured AI receptionist handles general enquiries and lead capture. It doesn't discuss vendor reserve prices, negotiate commissions, or share confidential deal information. When a call requires that level of sensitivity, the AI recognises it and routes to a human immediately.

"Will it work with Rex / PropertyMe / our current setup?"

This varies by provider and should be the first thing you verify. The best platforms integrate directly with major Australian real estate CRM and property management systems. Always confirm your specific software is supported — and test it — before committing. Check Entry's integrations.

"Our agency handles both sales and property management. Can one AI handle both?"

Yes. A properly configured AI receptionist can distinguish between sales enquiries and property management calls, applying different workflows to each. Buyer and seller leads go to the sales team; maintenance requests and tenant calls go to property management. The caller doesn't need to know which department they need — the AI figures it out.

Getting Started

  1. Track your missed calls for one week. Most phone systems can show you this data. Count how many calls go to voicemail or ring out — and what time of day they happen. The number will probably shock you. Pay particular attention to evenings, weekends, and Saturday open home hours.
  2. Calculate the cost. If you're missing 5 calls per day and even 10% are genuine seller or buyer leads, what's that worth in commission over a year? Most agencies find the number is six figures.
  3. Identify your must-have integrations. What CRM does your sales team use? Rex? VaultRE? What about property management — PropertyMe, Property Tree, Console? These are non-negotiable when evaluating providers.
  4. Start with after-hours and overflow calls. This is the lowest-risk approach. Keep your receptionist or agents answering during business hours, and let the AI capture everything that would otherwise go to voicemail. Get set up in minutes.
  5. Configure your lead routing. Tell the AI which agents cover which areas or listings, who handles property management, and what constitutes an urgent escalation. The more specific you are, the better it performs.
  6. Review and refine. Listen to call recordings in the first few weeks. You'll quickly identify where the AI needs more information about your listings, processes, or team structure. Most agencies are fully optimised within 2–3 weeks.
