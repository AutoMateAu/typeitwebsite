The drawing schedule problem

If you're a private certifier in Australia, you already know the pain. Every development application, every construction certificate, every complying development certificate comes with a stack of drawings. And for every stack, you need a drawing schedule.

Drawing schedules aren't optional. They're a critical compliance document that records every drawing in a package — the drawing number, revision, title, date, and status. Council needs them. Your records need them. Your clients expect them.

The problem? Creating them manually is tedious, time-consuming, and error-prone.

Most certifiers we've spoken to spend between 30 minutes and 2 hours per package just on drawing schedules alone. For firms processing 20+ jobs per month, that's 10–40 hours of admin work that adds zero value to the actual certification process.

What the manual process actually looks like

Let's break down what happens when a certifier receives a new package today:

  1. Download the documents — PDFs arrive via email, portal, or USB. Sometimes it's one combined file, sometimes it's 30 separate documents.
  2. Open each drawing — You need to check the title block on every single page to find the drawing number, title, revision, and date.
  3. Create a spreadsheet or table — Most certifiers use Excel or Word to list out every drawing with its details.
  4. Cross-reference and verify — Check that nothing's been missed, revision numbers match, and dates are consistent.
  5. Format for the certificate — The schedule needs to be formatted properly for attachment to the CC, OC, or CDC.

This process is repeated for every single job. And when drawings get revised mid-assessment (which happens constantly), you're updating the schedule again.
